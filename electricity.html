Dynamic Electricity

A. Electric Field

  • Negative charges have an electric field pointing inwards, positive charges have an electric field pointing outwards. Therefore, the positive charges move toward the negative charges and are repelled by another positive charge.

    • B. Electric Potential

  • Electric potential is the potential energy of of each charge
  • Let the electric potential of a given location be 10 Joules per Coulomb (J/), that means that for every Coulomb, there will be 10 Joules of potential energy for the charge. If there are 2 Coulombs, there would be 20 Joules of potential energy.

    • C. Electric Potential Difference

      - The unit of electric potential difference is (V) Volts/Voltage
      - 1V = 1J/C
      If the electric potential difference between two locations is 15 Volts, then 1 Coulomb of charge.
      If a circuit has a battery with 30V, then every Coulomb of charge gains 30 Joules every time it passes through the battery.

D. Relation Between Electric Field and Battery

  • A battery works the same as an electric field, the positive side flows to the negative, therefore charges passing through the will flow from the positive to the negative side.

    • E. Electric current

  • Electric current is the rate at which charge flows past a point in the circuit.
  • The standard unit for a current is the ampere(A)
      • 1 ampere = 1 coulomb per 1 second

    F. Resistor

  • The function of a resistor is to control or limit the electric current that flows in a circuit.
      • Electric resistance is a measure of how much the electrical component opposes/fights the passage of an electric current
      The unit for resistance is the ohm(Ω)
